Applications are open for 2026.

Baltimore Scores in STEM provides scholarship awards of $2,000* ($500 per year), in addition to ongoing mentorship, to one student each year to offset the expenses that student faces in pursuing their education in STEM.

Please download the application form below, fill it out, and email it with your resume and high school transcript with the subject "2026 Scholarship Application - Your Name" to [email protected].

*Award recipient will receive $500 per year for 4 years, contingent on maintaining a 3.0 GPA, pursuing a STEM degree, and remaining in good standing with their institution. Deadline: May 31, 2026. Recipient announced June 2026.

Eligibility

Recipients are selected based on:

Resume
High school transcript
Two short essays on athletics participation and STEM passion
Currently enrolled at a BCPS school
Planning to pursue a STEM degree at an accredited institution
NCAA Compliance

Open to NCAA athletes.

NCAA athletes of Division I, II, and III may receive this scholarship. Division I athletes should reference bylaw 15.2.6.4 regarding athletically related financial aid from an established and continuing program.

Key conditions:

Recipient's choice of institution is not restricted by the donor

No direct connection between donor and the student-athlete's institution

Scholarship is awarded on a basis other than athletic ability

Scholarship Recipients

Meet our past recipients.

2025 Recipient

Jaron Harris

Jaron is studying neuroscience at the University of Maryland. Football shaped much of his early life, from six years of youth league play to competing at Nationals as a defensive end. Growing up in Cherry Hill, he committed himself to expanding youth programming and mental health resources, creating Cortado — a virtual community of more than 8,000 members — during the pandemic. As a RAMP scholar at the University of Maryland School of Medicine, he conducted research on genetic determinants of cellular aggregation. He aims to become a physician-scientist who deepens our understanding of the brain and supports youth in under-resourced communities.

2025 Recipient

William Allen

William is beginning his freshman year at the University of Richmond, pursuing a path that bridges medicine and biomedical engineering. From hands-on research in the Hur Microfluidics Lab at Johns Hopkins — where he contributed to a published study — to shadowing physicians and volunteering at Johns Hopkins Hospital, William has consistently sought opportunities to grow. A lifelong swimmer, he credits the sport with teaching him consistency, teamwork, and resilience.

2023 Recipient

Ashton Mayeux

Ashton is studying cybersecurity and statistics at Bowie State University. He continues his lifelong enjoyment of studying mathematics and is interested in learning how he can contribute to technology development. Ashton was involved in numerous sports growing up, where he notes that "with each sport, there was something unique that [he] learned that helped [him] grow as a person."

2022 Recipient

Akil Smith

Akil is studying marine biology at the University of Miami. His interest developed through work at the National Aquarium in Baltimore. He aspires to further our understanding of ocean life and inspire greater representation of Black males in STEM fields. Akil attributes athletics to gaining "leadership, vision, creating goals, knowing when to react, and calling the right plays."
